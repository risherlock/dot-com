About me

I am Rishav (ऋषभ). Welcome to my website where I attempt to document my hobbies, tinkering, and researches. It took some time for me to realize that modern tools and methods of web design are too complex for me to understand and maintain, so I decided to go bare minimum which seems to serve my purpose (hopefully it does to the reader too).

I am genuinely astounded by, as Eugene Wigner put it, The unreasonable eﬀectiveness of mathematics as the language of nature. This is the reason I am inclined to the theoretical aspects of control and estimation theory and subsequent digital implementations. I have a passion for embedded systems and enjoy the processes involved; breadboard prototyping, PCB design, and writing embedded firmware.

My other interests include reading (in general), cooking, gardening, calligraphy, and playing violin.

Research motivations

I have special feelings for the application of differential geometry to applied control and estimation theory. Specifically, I believe that differential geometry can provide important insights on the propagation of stochastic variables through nonlinear dynamics leading to analytical nonlinear generalization of Kalman Filter. My interest in mathematical generalization of a theory is hugely inspired by Einstein's General Theory of Relativity.

I don't believe that a research goal should necessarily be directed towards difficult questions. On the contrary, I feel that deeper understanding of a topic can lead to humble problems whose solutions might provide profound insight to the subject under question. In this context, I often find myself going back to an excerpt from Michael Faraday's writing.

"It is the great beauty of our science that advancement in it, whether in a degree great or small, instead of exhausting the subjects of research, opens the doors to further and more abundant knowledge, overflowing with beauty and utility."

- Michael Faraday (Experimental Researches in Electricity, Vol. 2 (1834))

Furthermore, Sherlock Holmes, the great consulting detective, also makes a point in this line of reasoning that there is more to explore.

"The resources of science are far from being exhausted."

- Sherlock Homles to Dr. Watson on The Valley of Fear
